AI Interaction Standards for Schools

Declare conditions of AI use before interaction begins.

As AI becomes part of everyday learning, the central challenge is no longer whether it is used, but under what conditions.

These interaction standards allow schools to define roles, limits, and responsibility before students or staff engage with an AI system. This supports learning, safeguarding, and duty of care without monitoring or surveillance.

What these standards do

  • Declare how AI is allowed to respond in an educational context
  • Clarify what remains the responsibility of the student or teacher
  • Reduce ambiguity before AI use begins
  • Align with existing safeguarding and governance frameworks

Declared interaction contexts

Each context defines the conditions under which an AI system is permitted to operate within a school environment. Contexts may be selected or changed as instructional intent changes. Responsibility remains with the institution and supervising adults.

Child Context
Child-safe interaction context. Declares age-appropriate limits, tone, and scope before use.
Author / Writing
Supports writing while preserving student authorship.
Thinking / Clarity
Reasoning, analysis, and structured exploration.
Research / Evidence
Sources, verification, and factual grounding.
Everyday / Practical
Planning, communication, and routine support.
Gentle / Grounded
Calm, respectful, steady interaction.

Governance alignment

These interaction standards function as declared conditions of use. They do not monitor students, collect data, or control AI systems. They are designed to support existing policies, safeguarding practices, and institutional duty of care.
